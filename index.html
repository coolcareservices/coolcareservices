ABOUT ME

Air con can be one of the most important devices for your property today. You can install this unit for supporting your need every day. If you need to use your air conditioner for a long time, you want to learn about how to take care of this unit. There are some useful tips that you can follow, so you are able to maintain the quality of your air con properly. This article is going to talk about awesome facts on aircon servicing. These facts allow you to learn about how you can service or aircon repair any of your aircon units properly.
General Check-Up
One of the essential steps in aircon servicing is a general check-up of your unit. This includes inspecting the fan, coils, and other components for signs of wear and tear. Any parts showing signs of wear will need to be replaced before they cause further damage. In addition, your air conditioner will be checked for leaks and other potential problems.
Cleaning AC Filter
The AC filter is responsible for trapping dirt, dust, and other airborne particles. Over time, these particles can build up and cause your air conditioner to work harder than it needs to. When your AC is serviced, the technician will clean the filter and remove any buildup.
Vacuum Evaporator Coil
The evaporator coil is responsible for absorbing heat from the air. When this coil becomes dirty, it can’t absorb heat as effectively, and your air conditioner has to work harder to cool your home. The technician will vacuum the coil during aircon servicing to remove any dirt and dust./div>
Cleaning The Drain Pan And Pipe
The drain pipe carries away any water that condenses on the coils; over time, it can become clogged with dirt and debris. This can prevent proper drainage, leading to water damage and bacterial growth. To clean the drain pan and pipe, a technician will flush the pipe to remove any stuck debris and then thoroughly clean the pan. In some cases, chemicals may remove bacteria and other contaminants.
Inspecting Thermistor
The thermistor is a small electrical component that helps to regulate the temperature in your air conditioner. As the name suggests, it is similar to a thermostat and responsible for controlling the cooling and heating cycles in your AC unit. Our technicians will thoroughly inspect the thermistor during routine servicing to ensure it is properly adjusted. If we find that the thermistor is not functioning correctly, we will make the necessary adjustments to ensure that your AC unit is operating as efficiently as possible.
